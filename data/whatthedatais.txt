speakers.csv
------------
Hardware specs for every speaker in the basement DAC testbed, one row per
physical driver. Columns: zone_id, speaker_name, type, freq_min, freq_max,
sensitivity, impedance, power_handling, crossover_freq.

Pulled straight from the manufacturer datasheets (Polk, JBL, Klipsch, Micca,
Dayton). The crossover_freq column is only filled in for the subwoofer since
that's the only zone where it actually matters - the rest are full-range.

Zones in the testbed:
  2 - 4x Polk RC80i (overhead, two L/R pairs)
  3 - 2x JBL Stage A130 (bookshelf L/R pair)
  4 - 2x Klipsch R-41M (horizontal-firing L/R pair)
  5 - 2x Micca MB42X (horizontal-firing L/R pair)
  7 - 1x Dayton SUB-1200 (subwoofer)

training_data.csv
-----------------
The labeled data the KNN trains on. Each row is one song that we listened to
through one zone, with the bass and treble knobs set to where it sounded best
to us. Columns: zone_id, speaker_model, song_title, artist, volume, bass,
treble, clarity.

Built by hand over a bunch of listening sessions in the basement - same song
gets played through every zone, knobs get turned until it sounds right, values
get written down. The clarity column is our subjective 1-5 score for how good
the result actually sounded (used as a sanity check, not as a model input).

Bass and treble are in the controller's native -10 to +10 dB range. Volume is
the controller setting at the time, kept roughly constant across sessions so
it doesn't bias the tuning.

For zone 7 (subwoofer) the treble column is always 0 since the sub physically
can't reproduce treble.
